Journey To the West

Introduction

Journey to the West is a famous Chinese novel by Wu Cheng'en. It tells the story of the monk Tang Sanzang (Tripitaka) traveling to India for sacred scriptures, accompanied by Sun Wukong, Zhu Bajie, and Sha Wujing. The novel mixes adventure, fantasy, and religion, and focuses on self-discipline and egitnlightenment.

“A thousand hardships lie on the road to enlightenment, but the heart that endures will reach the truth.”
Journey to the West (Chinese: 西遊記; pinyin: Xīyóu Jì) is a Chinese novel published in the 16th century during the Ming dynasty and attributed to Wu Cheng'en. It is regarded as one of the great Chinese novels, and has been described as arguably the most popular literary work in East Asia.[2] It was widely known in English-speaking countries through the British scholar Arthur Waley's 1942 abridged translation Monkey.

Characters

Character Traits & Powers

  • Tang Sanzang: Kind, compassionate, wise
  • Sun Wukong: 72 transformations, magic staff, cloud-riding
  • Zhu Bajie: Lazy, loves food, humorous, uses nine-tooth rake
  • Sha Wujing: Calm, patient, loyal, uses monk's spade
