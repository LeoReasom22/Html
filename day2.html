Journey To the West

Introduction

Journey to the West is a famous Chinese novel by Wu Cheng'en. It tells the story of the monk Tang Sanzang (Tripitaka) traveling to India for sacred scriptures, accompanied by Sun Wukong, Zhu Bajie, and Sha Wujing. The novel mixes adventure, fantasy, and religion, focusing on self-discipline and enlightenment.

Read more about Journey to the West
“A thousand hardships lie on the road to enlightenment, but the heart that endures will reach the truth.”
Journey to the West (Chinese: 西遊記; pinyin: Xīyóu Jì) is a Chinese novel published in the 16th century during the Ming dynasty. It is one of the greatest classical Chinese novels and became famous in the English-speaking world through Arthur Waley’s translation Monkey.

Characters

Character Traits & Powers

  • Tang Sanzang: Kind, compassionate, wise
  • Sun Wukong: 72 transformations, magic staff, cloud-riding
  • Zhu Bajie: Lazy, humorous, uses nine-tooth rake
  • Sha Wujing: Calm, patient, loyal, uses monk’s spade
